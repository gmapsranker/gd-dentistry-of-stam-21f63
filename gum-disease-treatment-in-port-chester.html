Frequently Asked Questions About Gum Disease Treatment

How much does gum disease treatment typically cost at GD Dentistry of Stamford?
Treatment costs vary based on the severity of your gum disease and required procedures. Basic scaling and root planing (deep cleaning) typically ranges from $200-400 per quadrant. More advanced periodontal treatments may cost between $500-1,500. We accept most major insurance plans, which usually cover 50-80% of periodontal treatments. We also offer payment plans through CareCredit and can provide a detailed cost breakdown during your initial consultation.
How long does the gum disease treatment process take from start to finish?
The complete treatment timeline depends on your gum disease severity. For mild to moderate cases, we typically schedule 2-4 appointments over 4-6 weeks. Each deep cleaning session takes about 1-2 hours per quadrant. More severe cases may require 3-6 months of ongoing treatment. We'll create a personalized treatment schedule during your first visit to our Port Chester office, including maintenance cleanings every 3-4 months afterward to prevent recurrence.
Will my insurance cover gum disease treatment at your Port Chester location?
We work with most major insurance providers in the Westchester County area, including Delta Dental, Aetna, Cigna, and MetLife. Periodontal treatment is typically covered at 50-80% after meeting your deductible. Our office staff will verify your coverage before treatment and can provide a detailed breakdown of expected out-of-pocket costs. We'll submit all insurance claims on your behalf and help maximize your benefits.
Is the gum disease treatment painful? What type of anesthesia do you use?
We prioritize patient comfort during all treatments. Local anesthesia is always used during deep cleaning procedures. For anxious patients, we offer nitrous oxide (laughing gas) or oral sedation options. Dr. Garcia and our team use advanced ultrasonic cleaning equipment which is generally more comfortable than traditional scaling tools. Most patients report minimal discomfort during treatment and mild sensitivity for 1-2 days afterward, which can be managed with over-the-counter pain relievers.
What happens if gum disease returns after treatment? Do you offer any guarantees?
While we can't guarantee gum disease won't return, we provide comprehensive aftercare support to minimize this risk. This includes detailed home care instructions, recommended products, and scheduled maintenance cleanings every 3-4 months. If you maintain regular preventive appointments and follow our care instructions but experience recurring issues within 6 months of treatment, we'll provide additional cleaning at a reduced cost. The key to long-term success is consistent maintenance and good home care habits, which we'll help you develop.
